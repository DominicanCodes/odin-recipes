Sancocho

Description

This Caribbean stew is chock full of meat and veggies that are sure to satisfy your large apatite. Serve over rice and enjoy!

Ingredients

  • Pork, beef, chicken, or a combination of the three
  • Smoked pork neck bones
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Oregano
  • Adobo
  • Whole allspice
  • Sofrito (Here is a great recipe: link)
  • Chicken Bouillon
  • Lime
  • Vegetables (plantains, corn, carrots, yuca, yauita, auyama)
  • Herbs (cilantro, recao cilantro, thyme

Steps

  1. Trim meat fat and rinse with lemon juice or vinegar
  2. Season the meat with 1 teaspoon of adobo, 1/2 teaspoon of pepper, 1 teaspoon oregano and sofrito
  3. In a 6 quart pot, heat up oil over medium high heat
  4. Add meat to pot, sear until brown all around
  5. Cover the pot with a lid ad lower heat to medium
  6. Let the meat cook until tender, about 30 - 40 minutes
  7. Add water as needed throughout so that the meat doesn't burn
  8. While the meat cooks, peel and cut the vegetables into 1-inch pieces, except for the squash, which you want to peel and cut into two large pieces
  9. Remove meat from pot and set aside for later
  10. Add all the vegetables to the pot and add water until you've filled ¾ of the pot. Bring to a boil
  11. When the water begins to boil, add the chicken bouillon and allspice or malagueta. Let it cook for about 15 - 20 minutes
  12. When the squash is tender, remove it from the pot and blend it with ½ cup of liquid from the pot and ½ cup of cool water until the mixture is smooth. Then, pour it back into the pot
  13. sing cooking twine, tie together tightly cilantro, culantro (cilantro ancho), and thyme and add it to the pot. Let it simmer on medium heat for about 20 more minutes
  14. Stir in ½ teaspoon of adobo, ½ teaspoon of oregano, red pepper flakes, salt and pepper to taste
  15. Add the meat back into the pot and let it all simmer until the vegetables are tender, about 10 - 20 minutes
  16. With a colander, remove the herbs bundle we added earlier and the malagueta
  17. Stir in freshly squeezed lime juice before serving
  18. Serve warm with rice and fresh avocado

Buen Provecho! (Enjoy your meal!)
